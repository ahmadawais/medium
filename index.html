Accessibility ![A Nutshell](http://dl.dropbox.com/u/37626989/nutshell.jpg)_Core Ideas:_ Everyone needs to be able to access it, Else it's not going on the website. This includes: Blind People. Deaf People. People with old computers. People without _desktop_ computers People with dial up. People with motor issues. This is not hard... If you don't leave it to the end. We don't bite, we want to help you be able to do this. That being said we don't want to do it for you. Teach a fish etc. Usually this is your consultant's responsibility. Patrick put it into the contract boilerplate. _Think web first._ We are supposed to be GreenDOT. ![wasteful paper](http://dl.dropbox.com/u/37626989/waste%20paper%20real.jpg)_Do you like wasteing paper?_ How To: Add alt text to images as you make them. Your software supports this. Trust me it does. Alt text serves in place of an image. _Bad:_ A map showing cities by size in MA. _Good:_ Boston is the biggest city in MA. You can also tag images as background. Scanned PDFs are a no-no. Only Exceptions: Letters physically mailed to us. Documents that predate the digital era. Not Excuses: Documents your boss signed. Documents that MassDEP sent you. Always export to PDF or save as PDF _Never print to PDF!_ Keep it under 4MB. (If possible.) If it's not a report appendix is almost always possible. _speaking of complex reports_ instead of one giant unwieldy pdf you can give me a folder of pdfs labled 1-cover, 2-1-toc, 2-2-table of figures, etc and when in doubt you can get me the non-pdf version _Excel_ If you can't sort it, it's not accessible. It also needs to make sense if you: turn off all the styles, unsplit all the cells, and view a row out of context. _Power Point_ It's not for maps. It's not for posters. If it's not a presentation, your using it wrong. Also your template needs to be accessible too. Make sure your (alt tagged) graphics are sensibly sized. That poster the architect showed at your meeting? Don't bother mucking around in Acrobat. We can put the alt text on the web page next to the link to the PDF. _File Names_ "report aproved updated 08041946 FINAL.pdf" is fine for you as a title but for posting: No spaces, it can break links use CamelCase, snake_case, or spinal-case remove your book keeping notation e.g. "aproved", "final", the date etc. _Tips:_ Keep it simple. The visual flourishes, not the content are often the problem. Make it right first, then make it pretty. Touch base with us _before_ you start making it. "_I need the report I spent 8 months on made accessible and posted COB today._" Will not endear you to us. It's hard/impossible to fix a document already PDFed. If I'm going to help you I need the original. Which means _you_ need the original from your consultant. If your exporting to PDF and then bringing those PDFs into a document you plan on making into a PDF, stop and come talk to me. There is a life beyond Acrobat This presentation is in HTML It is ridiculously accessible. Even on your phone. _FIN_
